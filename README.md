<h2><strong>What Causes Vapes to Leak in the First Place?</strong></h2>
<p>Before we get into solutions, let's talk about why leaking happens at all.</p>
<h3><strong>Pressure Differentials</strong></h3>
<p>Think of your vape pod like a water bottle. When you're on a plane and the cabin pressure changes, what happens? The bottle crumples or the cap pops off. Same principle applies here.</p>
<p>Temperature swings create pressure changes inside the pod. Hot car dashboard? The liquid expands. Cold winter pocket? It contracts. These shifts push e-liquid through any available gap.</p>
<h3><strong>Poor Seal Design</strong></h3>
<p>This one's simple but critical. If the seal between the pod and device isn't tight, liquid finds a way out. It's like trying to keep water in a bucket with a crack &ndash; physics always wins.</p>
<h3><strong>Gravity and Orientation</strong></h3>
<p>Leave your vape upside down long enough, and gravity does its thing. Poorly designed airflow channels can become highways for escaping liquid.</p>

<h3><strong>COREX Heating Technology</strong></h3>
<p>Here's where things get interesting. Traditional coils heat unevenly, creating hot spots that can cause liquid to thin out and seep through gaps. <h2><strong>The Role of Airflow in Leak Prevention</strong></h2>

<h3><strong>Pressure Equalisation Channels</strong></h3>
<p>The <strong>vaporesso xros series</strong> includes tiny channels that balance internal and external pressure. When the pod heats up, these channels let air in or out without forcing liquid to move.</p>
<p>It's similar to how modern fuel tanks have venting systems. The pressure needs somewhere to go that isn't through your e-liquid reservoir.</p>
<h3><strong>MTL Airflow Design</strong></h3>
<p>Mouth-to-lung draw styles create less internal vacuum than direct-lung hits. The XROS series is optimised for MTL, which means less suction force pulling liquid where it shouldn't go.</p>

<p>Mesh coils aren't just for flavour &ndash; they're leak defenders too.</p>
<h3><strong>Even Saturation Distribution</strong></h3>
<p>Traditional wire coils have hotspots. Mesh spreads the heat across a larger surface area. This means the cotton saturates evenly rather than getting scorched in one spot and flooded in another.</p>
<p>Even saturation means controlled liquid flow. No dry hits, no flooding, no leaking.</p>
<h3><strong>Larger Surface Contact</strong></h3>
<p>More cotton touching more coil means better liquid retention. It's like the difference between a paper towel and a sponge &ndash; one holds significantly more without dripping.</p>
